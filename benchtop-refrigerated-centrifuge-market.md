<p><p>The Benchtop Refrigerated Centrifuge market is categorized into two main types: high-speed and low-speed centrifuges. High-speed benchtop refrigerated centrifuges operate at elevated rotational speeds, typically exceeding 15,000 RPM, making them ideal for applications requiring rapid separation of fine particles and cell cultures. In contrast, low-speed benchtop refrigerated centrifuges operate at lower RPMs, generally below 5,000 RPM, which are better suited for applications involving larger particles, such as blood components and sedimentation processes, where gentler separation is needed.</p></p>
