<p><p>The medical hand orthosis market comprises two main types: dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement and support functional activities while allowing some degree of joint mobility. They are typically used for rehabilitation and to improve hand function. In contrast, static orthotics provide rigid support and immobilization to stabilize injuries or conditions, promoting healing and preventing further damage. Both types serve crucial roles in hand therapy and recovery, catering to varying patient needs.</p></p>
